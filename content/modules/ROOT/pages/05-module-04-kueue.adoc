= Module 4: Kueue Concepts
:navtitle: Module 4: Kueue
:toc: macro
:toclevels: 1
:icons: font

https://kueue.sigs.k8s.io/docs/overview[Kueue^] is a Kubernetes-native job queueing system that manages resource quotas, fair sharing, and workload preemption. In this workshop environment, Kueue controls how GPU resources are shared between users with reservations and those using the unreserved pool.

In this module, you'll explore the Kueue resource hierarchy, understand how Cohorts enable resource sharing, and trace a workload through the admission process.

== Learning objectives

By the end of this module, you'll be able to:

* Describe the Kueue resource hierarchy: https://kueue.sigs.k8s.io/docs/concepts/resource_flavor[ResourceFlavor^], https://kueue.sigs.k8s.io/docs/concepts/cluster_queue[ClusterQueue^], https://kueue.sigs.k8s.io/docs/concepts/local_queue[LocalQueue^], https://kueue.sigs.k8s.io/docs/concepts/cohort[Cohort^]
* Explain how the unreserved Cohort enables borrowing and reclaiming
* Submit a workload and trace its admission through Kueue
* Identify when and why workloads are queued vs. admitted

== Kueue resource hierarchy

image::kueue-resource-hierarchy.png[Kueue Resource Hierarchy,link=self,window=blank,width=700]

The workshop cluster configures Kueue resources in a flat Cohort model:

----
Cohort: unreserved
├── ClusterQueue: unreserved        (borrows from cohort, no preemption rights)
├── ClusterQueue: unreserved-priority (can preempt low-priority borrowing workloads)
├── ClusterQueue: user-alice        (protected nominalQuota, can reclaim from cohort)
└── ClusterQueue: user-bob          (protected nominalQuota, can reclaim from cohort)
----

All ClusterQueues share resources through the **unreserved** Cohort. The key difference is in their preemption policies:

[cols="1,1,1,2",options="header"]
|===
| ClusterQueue | nominalQuota | Borrow? | Preempt?

| `unreserved`
| Total minus reserved
| Yes (from cohort)
| No -- workloads here are always preemptable

| `unreserved-priority`
| 0
| Yes (from cohort)
| Yes -- can preempt lower-priority borrowing workloads (see below)

| `user-<name>`
| User's reservation
| No
| Yes -- `reclaimWithinCohort: Any` reclaims its protected quota
|===

=== What is the difference between reclaiming and borrowing?

**Borrowing**: A ClusterQueue uses GPU/resource quota from another ClusterQueue in the same Cohort when its own quota is exhausted. The borrowing queue gets extra capacity it doesn't own.

**Reclaiming**: The queue that owns the quota takes it back from a borrower. When the owner needs its resources, it **preempts** workloads that were borrowing them.

In short: **borrowing** is "can I use your unused quota?", **reclaiming** is "I need my quota back."

=== What is low-priority borrowing?

The `unreserved-priority` ClusterQueue exists for workloads that need to preempt other unreserved work -- without having a reservation. It has `nominalQuota: 0` (no guaranteed resources), so all its resources are borrowed from the Cohort. The difference from the regular `unreserved` queue is its preemption policy:

[source,yaml]
----
preemption:
  borrowWithinCohort:
    policy: LowerPriority
    maxPriorityThreshold: 100
----

This means: "when I am borrowing from the Cohort, I can preempt other borrowing workloads -- but only those with a priority value of 100 or below." A workload using the `high-priority` WorkloadPriorityClass (value `10000`) submitted to `unreserved-priority` can preempt regular unreserved workloads (which have no priority class, defaulting to 0). But it still **cannot** preempt workloads running under a user reservation -- those are protected by `nominalQuota`, not borrowing.

This is useful for batch scheduling scenarios where certain unreserved jobs (e.g., urgent training runs) should jump ahead of background workloads without needing a full reservation.

=== What is protected quota?

When you make a reservation, the app creates Kueue resources that give your workloads **protected quota** -- they cannot be preempted by unreserved workloads.

We have configured reservation guarantees to ensure that user reservations are not preempted by unreserved workloads. This is a design choice to ensure that users can reserve GPUs for their own use and not have them taken away by other users whilst allowing unreserved workloads to run when resources are available.

The Booking App's design is achieved through the use of Kueue's Cohort model:

- the https://kueue.sigs.k8s.io/docs/concepts/cohort[Cohort^] (which is named `unreserved`) initially owns all the resources in the cluster
- all unreserved workloads are borrowing (their https://kueue.sigs.k8s.io/docs/concepts/cluster_queue[ClusterQueue^] has `nominalQuota: 0`).
- when a user makes a reservation, the Cohort is reduced by the amount of the reservation
- a user https://kueue.sigs.k8s.io/docs/concepts/cluster_queue[ClusterQueue^] `user-<name>` is created for the user with the `nominalQuota` matching the reservation
- their `user-<name>` https://kueue.sigs.k8s.io/docs/concepts/cluster_queue[ClusterQueue^] has `reclaimWithinCohort: Any` and `borrowWithinCohort: Never` which means it can preempt any workload borrowing from the cohort but never borrow itself

https://kueue.sigs.k8s.io/docs/concepts/preemption[Preemption^] works because the booking app actively shrinks the Cohort's capacity when quota is allocated. The unreserved CQ has 0 nominal quota (no guaranteed resources) and borrows from the Cohort. When the Cohort shrinks, the unreserved workloads are over their allocation, and the user CQ reclaims.

With this design, we can complement the Kueue Cohort model with other CQ's. One example (not covered in this workshop), is a https://kueue.sigs.k8s.io/docs/concepts/multikueue[Multi-Kueue^] environment. We have a CQ `cluster-queue` where batch workloads are assigned by a HUB Cluster (ACM) to SPOKE GPU enabled clusters. Unreserved workloads can then use the setting `borrowWithinCohort: LowerPriority` to preempt lower priority unreserved workloads - whilst preserving the reservation guarantees for users.

== Exercise 2: Explore ResourceFlavors and Topology

You should see three flavors:

[cols="1,3",options="header"]
|===
| ResourceFlavor | Purpose

. Inspect the `gpu-pool` ResourceFlavor:
+
[source,console,role=execute,subs=attributes+]
----

----
+
The key field is `spec.nodeLabels`:
+
[source,yaml]
----
spec:
  nodeLabels:
    run.ai/simulated-gpu-node-pool: default
----
+
This label selector is how Kueue knows which nodes have fake GPUs. When the GPU Config plugin labels worker nodes with `run.ai/simulated-gpu-node-pool=default`, those nodes become part of the `gpu-pool` flavor. Every ClusterQueue in the `unreserved` Cohort references this flavor in its `resourceGroups`, so all GPU workloads -- whether reserved or unreserved -- are scheduled onto these labeled nodes.

[source,console,role=execute,subs=attributes+]

----

https://kueue.sigs.k8s.io/docs/concepts/topology[Topology^] defines zone and hostname levels for pod spreading across nodes. We are using the default topology configuration which means that pods will be spread across nodes in the same zone and hostname.
+
You can use Topology to enable https://kueue.sigs.k8s.io/docs/concepts/topology_aware_scheduling[Topology Aware Scheduling^] by providing a model that represents the hierarchy of your organizational units (such as zones, blocks, and racks) along with node labels.

== Exercise 4: Understand the Cohort model

The https://kueue.sigs.k8s.io/docs/concepts/cohort[Cohort^] is the key to resource sharing. All https://kueue.sigs.k8s.io/docs/concepts/cluster_queue[ClusterQueues^] in the same Cohort share a single pool of resources.

[IMPORTANT]
====
**Acting as: `{user-user1}`** +
Log in to the OpenShift CLI:

[source,console,role=execute,subs=attributes+]
----
oc login --insecure-skip-tls-verify $(oc whoami --show-server) -u {user-user1} -p {password}
----
====

=== Cohort resources are ADDITIVE to CQ resources

A critical detail of Kueue's design: the total capacity available to a ClusterQueue is its own `nominalQuota` **plus** what it can borrow from the Cohort. They are additive, not overlapping. This has important implications for how you configure the system.

. Inspect the Cohort's resource totals:
+
[source,console,role=execute,subs=attributes+]
----
oc get cohort unreserved -o jsonpath='{range .spec.resourceGroups[0].flavors[0].resources[*]}{.name}: {.nominalQuota}{"\n"}{end}'
----

. Compare with the `unreserved` ClusterQueue's nominalQuota:
+
[source,console,role=execute,subs=attributes+]
----
oc get cq unreserved -o jsonpath='{range .spec.resourceGroups[0].flavors[0].resources[*]}{.name}: {.nominalQuota}{"\n"}{end}'
----
+
The `unreserved` CQ has `nominalQuota: 0` for every resource. It owns nothing -- all its capacity comes from borrowing from the Cohort. Now compare with the user CQ `user-{user-user1}` which is `reserved`:
+
[source,console,role=execute,subs=attributes+]
----
oc get cq user-{user-user1} -o jsonpath='{range .spec.resourceGroups[0].flavors[0].resources[*]}{.name}: {.nominalQuota}{"\n"}{end}'
----
+
With 1 GPU reserved, the three sources of quota look like this:
+
[cols="1,1,1,1",options="header"]
|===
| Resource | Cohort | CQ `unreserved` | CQ `user-{user-user1}`

| `nvidia.com/gpu`
| 7
| 0
| 1

| `nvidia.com/mig-1g.10gb`
| 56
| 0
| 0
|===
+
The cluster has 8 physical GPUs. The Cohort holds 7, the user CQ holds 1 -- total is 8. Because Cohort and CQ quotas are **additive**, the booking app must reduce the Cohort by exactly the reserved amount. If the CQ `unreserved` also had a non-zero nominalQuota, that would be added on top -- accidentally inflating the total beyond what the cluster actually has. The booking app avoids this by keeping all unreserved CQs at zero and putting the real capacity exclusively in the Cohort.

=== Why this matters for preemption

The additive model determines which preemption policies work:

* **`reclaimWithinCohort: Any`** -- only useful when a CQ has `nominalQuota > 0`. It reclaims resources that belong to the CQ but are being borrowed by others. With `nominalQuota: 0`, there is nothing to reclaim.

* **`borrowWithinCohort: LowerPriority`** -- useful when a CQ has `nominalQuota: 0` and needs to preempt other borrowers. Since everything is borrowed, preemption happens between borrowers based on priority.

This is why the booking app uses different preemption policies for different queue types:

[cols="1,1,1",options="header"]
|===
| ClusterQueue | nominalQuota | Preemption strategy

| `unreserved`
| 0
| None -- cannot preempt (nothing to reclaim, no priority preemption)

| `unreserved-priority`
| 0
| `borrowWithinCohort: LowerPriority` -- preempts lower-priority borrowers

| `user-<name>`
| User's reservation
| `reclaimWithinCohort: Any` -- reclaims its own protected quota from borrowers
|===

. Verify the Cohort shrinks when a reservation exists:
+
[source,console,role=execute,subs=attributes+]
----
oc get cohort unreserved -o jsonpath='{range .spec.resourceGroups[0].flavors[0].resources[*]}{.name}: {.nominalQuota}{"\n"}{end}' | grep gpu
----
+
If you created a reservation in Exercise 1, the Cohort's `nvidia.com/gpu` will show less than 8 -- the booking app reduced it by the reserved amount to prevent double-counting.

. Check the WorkloadPriorityClasses:
+
[source,console,role=execute,subs=attributes+]
----
oc get workloadpriorityclasses
----
+
Higher priority workloads are admitted first and can preempt lower priority ones (within preemption policy rules).

=== Verify

&#10003; Understand that Cohort and CQ nominalQuotas are additive +
&#10003; Unreserved CQs have `nominalQuota: 0` -- all capacity is in the Cohort +
&#10003; Reservation reduces Cohort quota to prevent capacity inflation +
&#10003; Preemption strategy depends on whether the CQ has nominalQuota or not +
&#10003; WorkloadPriorityClasses control admission order

= Getting Connected
:navtitle: Getting Connected

In this activity, you will connect to your workshop environment and verify access to the OpenShift cluster and OpenShift AI console.

== Log in to the OpenShift Console

[IMPORTANT]
====
**Acting as: `{user-admin}`** (cluster admin) +
Log in to the OpenShift Console at `{openshift_console_url}` with username `{user-admin}` and password `{password}`.
====

. Open the OpenShift Console in the right panel or navigate to:
+
`{openshift_console_url}`

. Log in with the following credentials:
+
**Username:** `{user-admin}` +
**Password:** `{password}`

== Log in from the command line

. Open a terminal in your environment.

. Log in to the OpenShift cluster:
+
[source,console,role=execute,subs=attributes+]
----
oc login --insecure-skip-tls-verify $(oc whoami --show-server) -u {user} -p {password}
----

. Verify your identity:
+
[source,console,role=execute,subs=attributes+]
----
oc whoami
----

== Verify GPU resources

. Check that fake GPU nodes are available:
+
[source,console,role=execute,subs=attributes+]
----
oc get nodes -l nvidia.com/gpu.present=true
----

. Verify GPU resources are allocatable:
+
[source,console,role=execute,subs=attributes+]
----
oc describe node -l nvidia.com/gpu.present=true | grep -A 10 "Allocatable:"
----
+
You should see `nvidia.com/gpu`, `nvidia.com/mig-1g.18gb`, `nvidia.com/mig-2g.35gb`, and `nvidia.com/mig-3g.71gb` in the allocatable resources.

== Verify Kueue is running

. Check Kueue controller pods:
+
[source,console,role=execute,subs=attributes+]
----
oc get pods -n openshift-kueue-operator
----

. List the ClusterQueues:
+
[source,console,role=execute,subs=attributes+]
----
oc get clusterqueues
----
+
You should see at least `default`, `unreserved`, and `unreserved-priority` ClusterQueues.

== Access OpenShift AI

. Navigate to the OpenShift AI dashboard:
+
`{openshift_ai_url}`
+
Or click **Red Hat OpenShift AI** in the right panel tabs.

. Verify you can see the Data Science Projects page.

== Verify your namespaces

. Verify your the user namespaces exists (user1, user2):
+
[.copypaste]
[source,bash,subs="attributes"]
----
oc get project {user-user1} {user-user2}
----
+
You should see the user namespaces `{user-user1}` and `{user-user2}`.

. Check LocalQueues in the user namespace:
+
[.copypaste]
[source,bash,subs="attributes"]
----
oc -n user-{user-user1} get localqueues
----
+
You should see `default` and `unreserved` LocalQueues.

=== Verify

You are ready to proceed when: +
&#10003; Logged into the OpenShift Console +
&#10003; `oc whoami` returns your username +
&#10003; Fake GPU nodes are visible with allocatable GPU resources +
&#10003; Kueue ClusterQueues exist +
&#10003; OpenShift AI dashboard is accessible +
&#10003; Your namespace has LocalQueues

== End of activity

You are now connected and ready to begin the workshop modules.
